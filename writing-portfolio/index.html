2. Quote Analysis

  • The Great Gatsby Chapter 1 - 3 Paragraph

    “His family were enormously wealthy - even in college his freedom with money was a matter for reproach…They had spent a year in France for no particular reason,” (p. 6). Fitzgerald clearly expresses his unrest with the massive wealth split being formed in the United States, which further takes away from the American Dream and the beginning promises of this country. Tom and Daisy have rarely worked in their life, and they chose to travel around Europe and burn money for having nothing better to do. Spending money like that exemplifies how some people should not have as much wealth as they do and share some of it with the struggling poor who wake up every morning and work hard all day for a few dollars.

  • Crime and Punishment I - II Assignment

    “‘Do they know everything, or do they still not know?...Mocking, sounding me out- and then all of a sudden they’ll come in and say they knew it all long ago…’” (Dostoevsky 122). The fear of his actions led Raskolnikov to believe that his conversation with his two friends were a set up and they knew about his murderous actions. In reality, they knew nothing, but the guilt made him believe that the talk was of himself, just as Chaucer said in his quote. The fear of being discovered for whatever one has done drives anxiety into the mind, and talk will suddenly trigger the notion that it is about them. Talk is perceived by the guilty as being about themselves because of the overload of fear. Raskolnikov, full of fear, was distraught in his quote because he believed Razumikhin and Zametov were onto him, exactly as Chaucer predicted.

  • Comparitive Essay

    “‘Jane Gallagher. Jesus’ ... I couldn't get her off my mind. I really couldn't. "I oughta go down and say hello to her, at least." "Why the hell don'tcha, instead of keep saying it?" Stradlater said. I walked over to the window, but you couldn't see out of it, it was so steamy from all the heat in the can.. "I'm not in the mood right now,"...You have to be in the mood for those things. […] I walked around the can for a little while. I didn't have anything else to do. (Salinger 42-43). The window directly coincides with Holden’s mood-lacking mindset of Jane. Holden, looking out the window, would have likely seen a campus with shady trees and bustling people, but that was tarnished by the intense steam from the bathroom. Stradlater had showered recently, and the window was becoming fogged up, similar to how Holden’s perfect view of Jane was no longer impeccable. From his childhood, Holden remembers Jane as the ideal girl, and their memories are encased in laughter and fun in his mind. However, deep down, he knows that she’s no longer the perfect girl for him when he rejects going down to see her, falsely claiming he is “not in the mood”. In Holden’s mind, she definitely has grown flaws he would despise because of his perfectionist attitude.

3. Reflection

  • My Progress

    I feel as though I have significantly progressing in my writing this year in many ways. First, I realized that it is not the length of the writing that helps explain one’s point and solidify the argument, it is the way you analyze the evidence and incorporate different English tactics into your writing in order to persuade the reader towards your point. This year I worked on improving my analytical skills of quotes, and not saying the typical “this quote means…” and then restating the plot. I have been delving deeper into the quotes and improving my skills at pulling apart and marking them up. In my Comparative Essay, I feel as though my quote markup was a drastic improvement from the beginning of the year, and I took different pieces of the quote and used them to prove my point to the reader. I feel more confident in my ability to markup a quote and develop an argument based off of what I find. Also, my writing has progressed in the revisionary stage I believe. In the past few years I have flown through an assignment and not bothered to go back and proofread my small essays, and this year I changed that bad habit. I took the time to go back, edit my writing, and shape it into a more cohesive, comprehensible piece that I was, in turn, much more proud of. This extra revisionary step helped me gain more knowledge of my writing errors and where I can improve myself when writing. The activity that helped me progress most in these aspects of my writing was the quote markup examples given in class. Going through a quote, breaking it apart, and sharing out ideas as a class was very beneficial in understanding how I can more effectively look at the quotes myself, and sharing ideas with others helped me to see new perspectives and ideas that I may have missed at a first glance. Overall, I believe my writing has improved greatly from the beginning of the year to now, and I am proud of the work I have done to better myself.

Overall Reflection - What have I learned as a writer?

  • Reflection on myself as a writer

    Reflecting back on all my work, I think my writing portfolio correctly showcases my growth over this year. One can clearly see how different aspects of my writing have grown, such as thesis and quote analysis. Coming into this class, my topic sentences were my thesis, my quotes were plopped, and my analysis was just scratching the surface. The Great Gatsby paragraph for chapters 5-6 is a clear example of my lack of skill in these categories. Looking through that paragraph I could see easy mistakes I would never make if writing a paper today, and this project was a great way for me to observe such growth. However, through wonderful teaching and working repeatedly on these skills, I’ve improved as a writer. My theses have gotten stronger, more arguable, and better worded, while my quote analysis now deciphers quote and relates it back to the claim. Over the course of the year, I’ve worked hard in class and outside of class to learn how to improve various areas of my writing. Going through the rest of my writing portfolio, my MEW and comma errors examples show I have a great grasp on how to effectively word and structure sentences now, and I’m confident in my abilities to fix such errors. With the major essay revision, I feel good about the changes I made to my thesis, topic sentences, quote integration, and the body paragraph from the Comparative Essay using the feedback I was given. Finally, the personal narrative and the creative piece are solid and pieces I’m particularly proud of.
    As a writer, I’ve learned many things about myself this year. First, formulating theses is a real struggle for me. I’ve gotten better at it, but instigating a strong argument that I can prove is not the easiest for me. It takes a few iterations and rewrites before I feel an acceptable thesis is created. In addition, I’ve learned that I get overwhelmed when writing long essays because I tend to be the type of person to just throw all my thoughts down at once and hammer out whatever assignment I’m presented with. Now that I understand that, I’ve realized I need to take breaks when I write essays and come back to it so I can space out my thoughts and really explain my claim in formulated, confident thoughts. When using this method my essays have been much cleaner and more understandable, and it has certainly helped me improve as a writer. Finally, I’ve become aware that if I focus more heavily on the outline process and building the evidence before I get to writing, creating a cohesive essay is much easier and the rough draft becomes a breeze. Moving forward, this information will massively help me write additional essays or assignments. For example, during senior year/college I know now how to write effectively theses, quote analyses and to slow myself down during the writing process to enhance the output.
    Putting all these factors together, I’m much more confident in my writing ability as a whole. When people edit my essay I feel so much more proud of the content I’m sharing with them. Parsing through old writing and projects from this class was very interesting, as I could clearly see my improvement and growth over time. I feel happy with my Comparative Essay and all the effort I put into that final paper, as it was an embodiment of all my hard work this year. This project was truly enlightening into my growth as a writer and what I need to do in the future to push myself to new heights. I’m very grateful for the skills this course has taught me and the knowledge I’ve gained through it; it’s been a long road, but one that was well worth it.
