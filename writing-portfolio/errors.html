MEW and Comma Errors

Eliminating Wordiness

Here are 10 examples of wordiness from my writing that I have corrected.

  1. Before: Having a support system around oneself to celebrate with through the exciting points of life but also pull one out of depression at the worst points is a crucial part of mental stability.
    After: Having a support system around oneself through the exciting points of life and the worst points is a crucial part of mental stability.
  2. Before: Whilst setting the bar for society so high, Holden makes it impossible for people to live up to his expectations, forcing him into isolation and depression.
    After: Whilst setting the bar for society so high, Holden forces himself into isolation and depression because no one can meet his expectations.
  3. Before: This inability to process the diversity of life leads him to distance himself from society as a whole, running away from his problems and in addition having spouts of depression.
    After: This inability to process the diversity of life leads him to run away from society and his problems while having spouts of depression.
  4. Before: His mother and father, noticing his lack of energy and will, try and reason with him in the hopes that he perks up, but their words offer little comfort to grief-stricken Hamlet.
    After: His mother and father try and reason with him, but their words offer little comfort to Hamlet.
  5. Before: The phrases “inky cloak” and “solemn black” bring depressing connotations, symbolizing that Hamlet did not process his father’s murder yet, and the despair is very much alive inside of him.
    After: The phrases “inky cloak” and “solemn black” bring depressing connotations, symbolizing that Hamlet did not process his father’s murder yet. The despair is very much alive inside of him.
  6. Before: Hamlet, unsure how to process his father’s death with his lying family around him not allowing him to outlet his depression, snaps at his mother.
    After: Hamlet snaps at his mother because he is unsure how to process his father’s death with his lying family around him.
  7. Before: Hamlet’s mother and stepfather are now proven liars to him, and he is surrounded by people who are fake, and that’s when he begins to gain madness.
    After: Hamlet begins to gain madness when his mother and stepfather are now proven liars and surround him.
  8. Before: He can not bear to see her again and have his wondrous memories of her damaged, so Holden says here that he is “not in the mood”, yet he has the perfect opportunity to go and see Jane.
    After: He can not bear to see her again and have his wondrous memories of her damaged, so Holden says here that he is “not in the mood”. However, he has the perfect opportunity to go and see Jane.
  9. Before: Wonderful female examples have been scarce so far in Hamlet’s life, so as his view of women turns negative it steers him to more madness, emphasizing the need for a different view from an outside source.
    After: With scarce examples of women his life, as Hamlet's view of women turns negative it steers him to more madness, emphasizing the need for a different view from an outside source.
  10. Before: Hamlet views women as entirely dependent on men, a view formed from his mother’s corrupt decisions which forced him into isolation and madness.
    After: Hamlet views women as entirely dependent on men; a view formed from his mother’s corrupt decisions which forced him into isolation and madness.

Fixing Comma Errors

Here are 10 examples of erraneous comma use from my writing that I have corrected.

  1. Before: At the highest heights one may feel unstoppable, and at the valley lows could be crawling for help.
    After: At the highest heights one may feel unstoppable and at the valley lows could be crawling for help.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  2. Before: Neither of these characters have outlets to pull them out of their despairs. They are lonely in their own little bubbles and facing massive disillusionment of life.
    After: Neither of these characters have outlets to pull them out of their despairs, so they are lonely in their own little bubbles and facing massive disillusionment of life.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  3. Before: Holden and Hamlet mentally deteriorate through a loss of interest in life when pushed into isolating themselves from others; however, Holden survives this spurt of uninterest through talking with Phoebe, while Hamlet accepts death because he lacks an outlet, highlighting the importance that to cope with the disillusionment of an imperfect life an outlet is necessary.
    After: Holden and Hamlet mentally deteriorate through a loss of interest in life when pushed into isolating themselves from others; however, Holden survives this spurt of uninterest through talking with Phoebe, while Hamlet accepts death because he lacks an outlet. This highlights the importance that to cope with the disillusionment of an imperfect life an outlet is necessary.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  4. Before: However, instead of overcoming this struggle to cope with imperfection, he recedes farther back into his mind, corrupting his own thoughts in the process.
    After: Instead of overcoming this struggle to cope with imperfection, he recedes farther back into his mind, corrupting his own thoughts in the process.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  5. Before: Rather than turn his focus to his school work and persevere, Holden participates in self-destruction through his hatred of phoniness as it “drives [him] crazy” and “makes [him] so depressed”.
    After: Holden participates in self-destruction through his hatred of phoniness as it “drives [him] crazy” and “makes [him] so depressed”, rather than turn his focus to his school work and persevere.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  6. Before: He wishes for everyone to be perfect humans, when in reality people lie and are fake.
    After: He wishes for everyone to be perfect humans when in reality people lie and are fake.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  7. Before: Holden rarely engages in good encounters with people, and wishes people could act honest towards each other; he exhibits how his past history has grasped his mind and is inciting insanity. Holden’s trapped in a whirlwind of lies around him, and no one’s there for him to scream the truth.
    After: Holden rarely engages in good encounters with people and wishes people could act honest towards each other; he exhibits how his past history has grasped his mind and is inciting insanity. Holden’s trapped in a whirlwind of lies around him, and no one’s there for him to scream the truth.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  8. Before: His mother and father, noticing his lack of energy and will, try and reason with him in the hopes that he perks up, but their words offer little comfort to grief-stricken Hamlet.
    After: Noticing his lack of energy and will, his mother and father try and reason with him in the hopes that he perks up, but their words offer little comfort to grief-stricken Hamlet.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  9. Before: “Cloak” is a word for a garment that hides the body, and the same is true for Hamlet’s thoughts.
    After: “Cloak” is a word for a garment that hides the body; the same is true for Hamlet’s thoughts.
    Explanation: Bleh.
  10. Before: Hamlet’s mother and stepfather are now proven liars to him, and he is surrounded by people who are fake, and that’s when he begins to gain madness.
    After: Hamlet’s mother and stepfather are now proven liars that surround him, and that is when he begins to gain madness.
    Explanation: Bleh.
